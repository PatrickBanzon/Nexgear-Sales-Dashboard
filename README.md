  <h1 align="center">Company Background</h1>

NexGear is an ecommerce company founded in January 2019, the company is focused on selling electronics gadgets and computer peripherals worldwide through its website and mobile app. 

In recent years the company has seen explosive growth (2019 – 2021) in sales and is assessing if the growth is sustainable or should the company expect sales to plateau or even decline. Other regions such as South America and Asia have been growing but market share remains to be small. A couple of products have been performing exceptionally but the rest of the rest of the product lineup contributes minimally to revenue.

Insights and recommendations are provided on the following key areas:
-	Sales trend analysis
-	Product level performance

**Executive Summary**

Starting from the Q1 of 2020 the overall sales of the business have risen to unprecedented levels that it already has eclipsed the previous years peak period. The surge in demand is overwhelmingly due to outside forces as the company and the industry overall have not made any significant change but has only adapted delivery services as part of Covid 19 restrictions.

**Sales Trends**

•	The total revenue of the company peaked in the Q4 of 2020 at $1.1M with $0.27M being the lowest revenue. The steady rise in revenue in 2019 can be attributed to stable and consistent growth in the overall market share while the growth in 2020 is mostly attributed to the pandemic and the shift to deliveries.

•	The company has seen a remarkable Quarterly average growth rate of 5.53% starting in 2019, this consumer demand is largely driven by outside factors which are not sustainable for the long-term. 

•	Overall direction of the total revenue leans towards a flat growth rate as the Q1 of 2021 compared to Q1 of 2020 shows a negative 25% growth rate signaling that post pandemic changes are negatively affecting the willingness and ability of consumers to buy our products.

**Product Performance**

•	TThe overwhelming share of revenue comes from only 3 products, with a combined revenue share comes to $4.7M or 92.5% total of overall sales. These are 27 inches 4K gaming monitor, Nintendo Switch, and Sony PlayStation 5 Bundle. The lack of diversification in products makes it more likely that a drop in a single product in revenue will have significant to severe effects in total revenue.

•	The following products have each contributed 1% or less of total sales: JBL Quantum 100 Gaming Headset, Acer Nitro V Gaming Laptop, Dell Gaming Mouse, and Razer Pro Gaming Headset. Even with the overall revenue growth, their individual revenue shares remain minimal to negligible.

•	In terms of marketing channel, direct sales account for 79.5% products sold while affiliate, email and social media are falling behind expectated growth rate. Strategy and overall plans need to be revised.

**Recommendations**

•	Diversify Revenue Streams The pandemic-driven surge is clearly unsustainable. The company should explore new product lines, complementary services, or adjacent markets to reduce over-reliance on external demand drivers.

•	Prioritize Customer Retention The -25% YoY decline in Q1 2021 vs Q1 2020 is a red flag. A loyalty program, re-engagement campaigns, and post-purchase follow-ups should be implemented immediately to slow the erosion of the customer base built during the pandemic boom.

•	Stabilize the Revenue Floor The lowest recorded quarter was $0.27M, which represents significant volatility. Subscription models, recurring orders, or B2B contracts could help create a more predictable revenue baseline and reduce dependence on seasonal or event-driven spikes.

•	Reduce Concentration Risk Immediately With 92.5% of revenue tied to just 3 products, the business is highly vulnerable. A demand shift in any one of these could severely damage overall revenue. The priority should be identifying and nurturing the next tier of products to carry more revenue weight.

•	Expand the Product Catalogue Strategically The current catalogue skews heavily toward big-ticket hardware. Adding mid-range accessories, peripherals, or gaming lifestyle products could attract a broader customer base, increase basket size, and distribute revenue risk more evenly.

•	With 85% of orders and 70% of revenue coming from just three products, diversifying the product portfolio is crucial. Expanding the accessory category with new product lines, particularly Apple charging cables, would provide upsell opportunities.
